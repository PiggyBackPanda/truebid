Selling a home privately can feel like navigating something designed to keep you in the dark. You take calls one at a time, buyers play coy about what they're willing to pay, and you're left wondering whether the offer in your hand is the best one or just the boldest. Traditional private negotiation favours buyers who know how to work the process: they know to lowball early, they understand that a seller sitting on the market for six weeks is desperate, and they've learned that you'll never know what you missed. Open Offers changes the rules.

## What Open Offers changes

With Open Offers, every offer made on your property is publicly visible on the listing page. Buyers can see what others have offered -- the amount and timing -- so they know exactly where they stand. If they want the property, they know what it takes. You, as the seller, watch genuine competition unfold in real time on your dashboard rather than piecing together information from separate conversations. The process stays transparent throughout.

One important distinction: this is not an auction. There is no auctioneer, no auction licence requirement, no falling hammer, and no obligation to sell at the end of the offer window. You retain full control over whether to accept any offer at all.

## Step-by-step seller walkthrough

**1. List your property and set your Open Offers window.**
Once your listing is published, you set the date and time when the offer window opens and when it closes. This is the period during which buyers can submit offers. The window can run for days or weeks -- you choose what suits your timeline.

**2. Buyers submit offers through the platform.**
Any verified buyer can submit an offer during the open window. Offers are displayed publicly on your listing page showing the amount and when it was submitted. Conditions attached to offers (such as finance clauses or settlement dates) are visible to you in your seller dashboard, but only the offer amount is shown publicly to other buyers.

**3. You watch the board in real time.**
Your seller dashboard shows every offer as it comes in, including the buyer's full details -- name, contact number, and email address. You can see the full picture at any time during the window, not just at the end.

**4. Buyers can increase their offer at any time.**
If a buyer sees they've been outbid, they can revise their offer upward during the open window. Offers can only go up, never down. Once submitted, an offer cannot be reduced. A buyer can withdraw their offer, but that withdrawal is recorded on their account.

**5. Anti-snipe protection keeps the process honest.**
If a new or updated offer comes in within the final 10 minutes of the offer window, the closing time automatically extends by 10 minutes from that moment. This keeps resetting as long as new activity happens in the final window. The offer period ends only when 10 minutes pass with no new offers, not just when the clock ticks over. This stops buyers from sitting on their hands until the last second and winning without genuine competition.

**6. When the window closes, you choose.**
Once the offer period ends, you review all offers and decide which one to accept -- if any. Price is the most visible factor, but you can weigh up conditions, settlement timeframes, and finance clauses. There is no pressure to rush this decision.

**7. You are never obligated to accept.**
If the highest offer doesn't meet your expectations, you don't have to take it. You can let the process close without accepting and decide your next step from there -- whether that's relisting, reducing your price expectations, or taking it off the market entirely.

**8. Acceptance hands over to your conveyancer.**
Once you accept an offer, your conveyancer handles the formal contract and settlement process from that point. TrueBid's role in the transaction is complete once you've made your selection.

## What buyers see

From the buyer's side, the Open Offers board on your listing page shows all active offers ranked by amount, with timestamps. Buyers see offer amounts and submission times. They do not see other buyers' personal details or the specific conditions attached to other offers -- only amounts and timing are visible to the public. This gives buyers enough information to make an informed decision about whether and how much to offer, while protecting offer details that are commercially sensitive.

The transparency tends to attract more serious buyers. Casual lowballers are less likely to submit when they can see genuine competition on the board.

## What Open Offers is not

Open Offers is not an auction. There is no auctioneer, no auction licence required from you as the seller, no compulsion to sell, and no public event with people standing on your front lawn. It is not a tender process -- offers are visible rather than sealed. It is not sealed offers where buyers submit once and hope. It occupies its own category: a structured, transparent offer period that brings market competition into the open without the theatre or legal overhead of a traditional auction.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can I set a reserve price?**
You can set a minimum offer threshold in your listing settings. Offers below this amount won't appear on the public board, though you'll still see them in your dashboard. This acts as a minimum offer threshold without the legal obligations that come with a formal auction reserve.

**Can I cancel the process if I change my mind?**
Yes. If your listing is active or under offer, you can withdraw it from your seller dashboard at any time. When you withdraw, all active offers on the listing are expired automatically and buyers are notified. The withdrawal is recorded on your account. You cannot withdraw a listing that has already been sold.

**What if only one buyer makes an offer?**
You still have full control. One offer is one more than you had before the window opened. You can accept it, decline it, or let the window close without responding. There is no obligation to sell simply because an offer was made.

**Can I negotiate privately with a buyer after the window closes?**
Once the offer window closes, you can communicate directly with any buyer whose offer you are considering. The platform does not prevent private discussion after the window ends -- that is a normal part of finalising terms before formal contracts are exchanged.

The goal of Open Offers is straightforward: let the market find the right price without the opacity of private negotiation or the pressure of a live auction. For sellers, that means going into the process confident that you've seen what the market is actually willing to pay -- and that you made your decision with the full picture in front of you, not just the version one buyer wanted you to see.
