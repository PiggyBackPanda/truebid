Commission rates don't sound that dramatic as percentages. In dollar terms, they're harder to ignore.

## What commission actually costs

Let's use two common property price points and run the numbers across typical commission rates.

### $750,000 property

| Commission rate | Commission cost |
|----------------|----------------|
| 1.5%           | $11,250        |
| 2.0%           | $15,000        |
| 2.5%           | $18,750        |

### $1,000,000 property

| Commission rate | Commission cost |
|----------------|----------------|
| 1.5%           | $15,000        |
| 2.0%           | $20,000        |
| 2.5%           | $25,000        |

Commission rates vary by state, agent, and market. In Perth and most of WA, rates tend to sit around 2% to 2.5% for metropolitan properties. In Sydney and Melbourne, negotiated rates can sometimes come in lower for higher-value properties.

These figures are GST-inclusive in most cases, but confirm that with your agent, as some quote ex-GST.

## Full cost of selling with an agent

**Commission:** As above. This is the biggest single cost by a significant margin.

**Vendor-paid advertising (VPA):** Many agents ask sellers to pay for marketing on top of commission. This covers realestate.com.au and Domain listings, social media ads, and sometimes print. Budget $2,000 to $5,000 depending on the campaign and the agent. Some agents include basic marketing; most charge separately for premium placement.

**Conveyancing:** You need a conveyancer regardless of how you sell. Expect $1,500 to $2,500 for a standard residential sale in WA, more in other states or for complex situations.

**Presentation costs:** These are optional but common: professional cleaning, minor repairs, garden tidy. Budget whatever your property needs, from a few hundred dollars upward. Agents often suggest these because a well-presented property typically sells faster.

**Total with an agent (rough range):** For a $750,000 property at 2% commission, you're looking at roughly $17,000 to $25,000 all in before any presentation work.

## Full cost of selling privately

**Photography:** $200 to $500 for a professional real estate photographer. Worth doing properly.

**Portal listings:** To list on realestate.com.au as a private seller, you'll use a third-party service. These typically charge $300 to $700 for a standard listing package. Domain has similar options. This isn't free, but it's a fraction of VPA through an agent.

**Conveyancing:** Same cost as above. $1,500 to $2,500.

**TrueBid:** Currently free to list and run your sale during our launch period. Registered users will receive at least 30 days notice before any fees are introduced.

**Presentation costs:** The same as with an agent. If your home needs work before going to market, that cost doesn't disappear.

**Total selling privately (rough range):** For the same $750,000 property, you're looking at roughly $2,500 to $4,000 all in, depending on what marketing you choose to do.

## What the numbers don't tell you

Private selling is not cost-free, but the saving is real and significant for most properties.

The question worth sitting with isn't really about the money. It's whether you're comfortable running the process yourself: showing buyers through, handling enquiries, understanding the paperwork, and coordinating with your conveyancer.

For some sellers, the commission is worth it because they genuinely don't want to manage those things. For others, the process is straightforward enough that paying tens of thousands of dollars for someone else to do it doesn't make sense.
