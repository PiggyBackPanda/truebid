## Section 1: For Sellers

**Heading:** How to sell your home on TrueBid

**Step 1 — Create your listing (15 minutes)**
Sign up and verify your identity, then enter your property details — address, bedrooms, bathrooms, land size, and a description of your home. Upload your best photos (your phone camera is fine — but good lighting makes a big difference). Choose your sale method: Open Offers for transparent bidding, Private Offers if you prefer discretion, or Fixed Price if you know exactly what you want.

**Step 2 — Go live and attract buyers**
Hit publish and your listing is live on TrueBid immediately. Buyers can find your property through our search, browse the details, and — for Open Offers — see the live offer board. You manage everything from your seller dashboard: track views, respond to questions, and watch offers come in.

**Step 3 — Review offers and accept**
When your Open Offers closing date arrives (or whenever you're ready), review all offers in your dashboard. You'll see each buyer's offer amount, their conditions, and their preferred settlement timeline. You choose the offer that suits you best — it doesn't have to be the highest. An unconditional offer with a quick settlement might be worth more to you than a higher offer subject to finance.

**Step 4 — Settle with your conveyancer**
Once you accept an offer, your settlement agent (conveyancer) prepares the Contract of Sale. The buyer arranges their deposit, inspections, and finance. Both parties sign, and your settlement agent handles the transfer of title. We provide a checklist to guide you through every step.

## Section 2: For Buyers

**Heading:** How to buy on TrueBid

**Step 1 — Browse and search**
Find properties by suburb, postcode, or address. Filter by price, bedrooms, property type, and sale method. No sign-up needed to browse — see every listing, every photo, and every offer on the board.

**Step 2 — Verify and place an offer**
When you find a property you love, verify your identity (takes 2 minutes with your driver's licence) and place your offer. For Open Offers listings, you'll see exactly where your offer ranks against other buyers — no guesswork, no phantom offers, no games.

**Step 3 — Compete transparently**
You can see every other offer: the amount, the conditions, and the timing. If you're outbid, you'll be notified instantly so you can decide whether to raise your offer. You compete on both price and terms — an unconditional offer might win over a higher offer with conditions attached.

**Step 4 — Win and settle**
If the seller accepts your offer, you'll be notified immediately. From there, engage your own settlement agent, arrange your building and pest inspection (if conditional), finalise your finance, and proceed to exchange and settlement. We guide you through the steps.

## Section 3: About Open Offers

**Heading:** What is Open Offers?

**Opening paragraph:**
Open Offers is TrueBid's transparent bidding system. Think of it as an auction — but fairer, less stressful, and open for weeks instead of minutes.

**How it works:**
When a seller lists their property with Open Offers, they set a closing date (typically 2–4 weeks away). During that time, any verified buyer can place an offer. Every offer appears on a live, public board showing the amount, the buyer's conditions (unconditional, subject to finance, etc.), and when it was placed. Buyers are identified by anonymous aliases — "Buyer_7a3k" — so your identity is protected, but the offer is transparent.

**What makes it different from a traditional auction?**
At a traditional auction, you have minutes to make a decision under extreme pressure, in a crowd of strangers, with an auctioneer pushing the pace. With Open Offers, you have days or weeks to think. You can arrange your finance, do your research, consult your family, and decide what the property is truly worth to you. There's no auctioneer, no crowd pressure, and no hidden reserve price games.

**What about sniping?**
Our anti-snipe protection means no one can swoop in at the last second. If a new offer is placed within 15 minutes of the closing time, the clock automatically extends by 15 minutes — just like a live auction where bidding continues as long as there's activity. The auction ends when the bidding stops, not when the clock stops.

**Does the seller have to accept the highest offer?**
No. The seller can accept any offer — including a lower one with better conditions. An unconditional offer with a 30-day settlement might be more valuable than a higher offer subject to finance with a 90-day settlement. This is exactly how real property transactions work — TrueBid just makes it visible.

**Is it free?**
Yes. Listing on TrueBid and using Open Offers is completely free. No commission, no listing fee, no hidden charges. We make money through optional premium services and referral partnerships with conveyancers and inspectors — services you'd use regardless of how you sell.

**Is it really free to list my property?**
Yes. Creating an account, verifying your identity, and listing your property on TrueBid is completely free. There's no listing fee, no commission, and no hidden charges. We offer optional paid services (like listing on realestate.com.au and Domain for $149) but the core platform is free.

**How does TrueBid make money if it's free?**
We earn revenue through optional premium listing upgrades and referral partnerships. When you need a settlement agent, building inspector, or mortgage broker, we can connect you with trusted partners — and they pay us a small referral fee. These are services you'd need regardless of how you sell, and you're never obligated to use our partners.

**Do I need a real estate agent to sell my property?**
No. In every Australian state and territory, it is legal to sell your own property without an agent. You do need a settlement agent (WA) or conveyancer/solicitor (other states) to handle the legal paperwork — but that's a fraction of the cost of an agent's commission. Our legal checklist guides you through exactly what's needed in your state.

**What is Open Offers?**
Open Offers is our transparent bidding system. Buyers place offers publicly, visible to everyone, with a closing date set by the seller. It's like an auction but without the pressure, the auctioneer, or the 60-second time limit. Buyers have days or weeks to decide, and the seller can accept any offer — not just the highest.

**Can buyers see my name on the offer board?**
No. Buyers are shown as anonymous aliases on the public offer board (e.g., "Buyer_7a3k"). Only the seller sees the buyer's real name, email, and phone number in their private dashboard.

**What if someone places a fake offer?**
Every buyer must verify their identity with a government-issued ID before they can place an offer. This prevents fake accounts and phantom bids. We also have systems in place to detect and prevent shill bidding (sellers bidding on their own property).

**What is the anti-snipe protection?**
If a new offer is placed within 15 minutes of the closing time, the closing is automatically extended by 15 minutes. This prevents "sniping" — waiting until the last second to bid — and ensures every buyer has a fair chance to respond.

**Do I have to accept the highest offer?**
No. You can accept any offer for any reason. Many sellers choose a lower offer with better conditions (unconditional, faster settlement, fewer risks). The choice is entirely yours.

**What happens after I accept an offer?**
Both you and the buyer are notified. Your settlement agent prepares the Contract of Sale with the agreed terms. The buyer arranges their deposit (held by a settlement agent's trust account — never by TrueBid), completes any inspections, and finalises finance. Both parties sign, and your settlement agent handles settlement and title transfer.

**Can I list on realestate.com.au and Domain?**
Yes, as an optional paid upgrade ($149). This pushes your listing to the major portals where most buyers search. Your TrueBid listing remains free regardless.

**What if my property doesn't sell?**
There's no time limit on your listing — it stays active until you sell, withdraw, or (for Open Offers) the closing period ends. If the closing period ends without you accepting an offer, you can re-list at any time for free.

**Is TrueBid available outside Western Australia?**
We're launching in Western Australia first. Expansion to Queensland, New South Wales, Victoria, and other states is planned for later in 2026. The legal requirements for private sales vary by state, and we're building state-specific compliance for each market.

**How do I know what my property is worth?**
Check recent sales of similar properties in your area on realestate.com.au (search "sold" properties in your suburb). You can also order a formal valuation from a licensed valuer ($300–$600), or use free online estimate tools. TrueBid's Open Offers system will also reveal the true market value through competitive bidding.

**What if I change my mind after listing?**
You can withdraw your listing at any time from your seller dashboard. If you have active offers, the buyers will be notified that the listing has been withdrawn. There's no penalty for withdrawing.

**Do I need a settlement agent before listing?**
You don't need one to list, but you should engage one before accepting an offer. We recommend arranging this early so you're ready to move quickly when the right offer comes in. Our checklist reminds you of this and can connect you with local settlement agents.

**Is my personal information safe?**
We take privacy seriously. Your identity verification is processed by a secure third-party provider — TrueBid never stores your ID documents. Your personal details (name, phone, email) are only visible to sellers when you place an offer. The public only sees your anonymous alias. See our Privacy Policy for full details.

**How is this different from BuyMyPlace or ForSaleByOwner?**
Those platforms charge $700–$2,000 upfront to list your property on realestate.com.au and Domain. TrueBid is free. More importantly, those platforms only handle the listing — you're on your own for managing offers and negotiations. TrueBid's Open Offers system gives you a structured, transparent way to receive and manage offers, creating competitive tension that typically achieves better prices.
